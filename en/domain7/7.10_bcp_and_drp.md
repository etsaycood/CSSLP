# 7.10 Business Continuity (BCP) and Disaster Recovery (DRP)

## Learning Objectives

- Distinguish between BCP and DRP
- Explain the role of the Business Impact Analysis (BIA)
- Describe the various types of Disaster Recovery Sites (Hot, Warm, Cold)
- Evaluate methods for testing recovery plans

---

## BCP vs. DRP

Security is not just about confidentiality and integrity; maintaining **Availability** in the face of natural disasters or severe cyberattacks is equally critical.

| Concept | Purpose | Scope | Timeline |
|---------|---------|-------|----------|
| **BCP** (Business Continuity Plan) | How does the *entire business* keep functioning during a disaster? | Broad (Operations, Personnel, Facilities, IT). e.g., "Where do the accountants work if the building burns down?" | Long-term strategic planning. |
| **DRP** (Disaster Recovery Plan) | How does the *IT department* restore the critical servers and data? | Narrow (IT specifically). e.g., "How do we failover the database to the secondary datacenter?" | Short-term tactical execution. |

> **Relationship**: The DRP is a highly technical subcomponent of the overarching BCP.

---

## The Business Impact Analysis (BIA)

Both plans rely entirely on the BIA. Security and IT teams *cannot* write the BCP or DRP in a vacuum. Management must conduct the BIA first.

- **Purpose**: Identify the organization's critical business functions, the IT systems that support them, and the financial/operational impact if those systems fail.
- **Output**: 
    1. **Prioritization**: Ranks systems (Tier 1 Critical, Tier 2 Important, Tier 3 Non-essential). You do not spend $1M failing over a cafeteria menu server.
    2. **Metrics**: Determines the **RTO** (how fast must we recover) and **RPO** (how much data can we lose).

---

## Disaster Recovery Sites

Based on the RTO determined by the BIA, the organization must select a recovery site strategy.

| Site Type | Description | Setup Time (RTO) | Cost |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|------|
| **Hot Site** | Fully equipped facility with identical hardware, software, and real-time replicated data. | Minutes / Hours | **Extremely High** |
| **Warm Site** | Facility with power, cooling, and basic networking/hardware. Data is NOT replicated in real-time. Tape backups must be physically delivered and restored. | Days / Weeks | Moderate |
| **Cold Site** | An empty room with power and cooling. You must buy new servers, install the OS, connect networks, and restore backups. | Weeks / Months | Low |

### Cloud Failover (Active-Active / Active-Passive)
Modern cloud architecture often renders traditional Hot/Cold sites obsolete.
- **Active-Active**: The application runs simultaneously in two separate regions (e.g., AWS us-east-1 and us-west-2). If the East region fails, the load balancer instantly sends 100% of traffic to the West region. **Near-zero downtime.**
- **Active-Passive**: The application runs in the primary region, while the secondary region is kept on standby (minimal resources provisioned). If primary fails, traffic is routed to the secondary, and auto-scaling spins up the necessary servers.

## Testing the DRP

A DRP that has never been tested is guaranteed to fail during a real disaster. Testing must occur regularly (e.g., annually).

| Test Type | Description | Disruptiveness |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Checklist / Desk Check** | Planners review the printed DRP document to see if phone numbers are still accurate and procedures make logical sense. | None |
| **Tabletop Exercise** | Key IT and business leaders sit in a conference room and verbally step through a disaster scenario (e.g., "Ransomware takes out Tier 1"). | Low |
| **Simulation** | Similar to tabletop, but involves actual incident response personnel practicing their tasks without impacting production (e.g., verifying they can log into the secondary region). | Low/Moderate |
| **Parallel Testing** | The failover site is activated and processes live traffic, but the primary site continues to operate normally. Both are checked to ensure they produce the same results. | Moderate |
| **Full Interruption (Failover)** | **The riskiest test.** The primary datacenter is physically disconnected or shut down. The entire business is forced to use the DR site. | **Extremely High** |

---

## Exam Focus Points

1. **BIA is paramount**: A BCP or DRP cannot be created without Management first conducting a BIA to identify maximum tolerable downtime (RTO) and data loss (RPO).
2. **DRP testing**: Parallel and Full Interruption are the only tests that actually prove the DR site works, but Full Interruption is rarely performed due to the risk of accidentally causing an actual disaster.
3. **Hot/Warm/Cold**: Hot sites use data replication (minutes). Warm sites require backups to be restored (days). Cold sites require hardware (weeks).

## Key Terms Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **BCP (Business Continuity Plan)** | Strategic plan for the entire organization to operate during a severe disruption |
| **DRP (Disaster Recovery Plan)** | Tactical IT plan for restoring technical operations |
| **BIA (Business Impact Analysis)** | Management exercise determining the criticality of business processes and establishing metrics (RTO/RPO) |
| **Hot Site** | Fully redundant, real-time data replication facility |
| **Warm Site** | Facility with hardware but requiring backup restoration |
| **Cold Site** | Empty facility lacking servers and data |
| **Tabletop Exercise** | Verbal discussion scenario to practice response without technical disruption |
